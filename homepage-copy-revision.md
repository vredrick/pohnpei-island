## SECTION 2 — ABOUT / INTRO

### Current
```
Enjoy the Purity of Pohnpei

Upon a stone altar, a place held close.

The island spans 133 square miles and stretches about 13 miles across,
resting just north of the equator in the heart of the Pacific. With an
average of 350 inches of rainfall each year, Pohnpei's mountains feed 
lush rainforests, winding rivers, and more than 40 breathtaking 
waterfalls. A vibrant coral reef encircles the island, sheltering a 
calm inner lagoon, while dense mangrove forests line the coast — 
protecting the shoreline and supporting generations of island life. 
Here, nature is not just scenery; it is part of everyday living.
```

### Revised
```
About Pohnpei

Pohnpei means "upon a stone altar."

Thirteen miles across, surrounded by reef, and almost always under 
rain. Pohnpei sits just north of the equator in the Caroline Islands 
— the capital of the Federated States of Micronesia and the wettest 
place in the Pacific. 

That rain is the reason everything here is alive. It feeds over 40 
waterfalls, rivers that cut through volcanic rock, and a rainforest 
so thick you can hear it breathe. A barrier reef wraps the entire 
island, holding a calm lagoon inside. Mangroves line the coast. The 
soil grows everything.

People have lived here for over 2,000 years. The culture — the 
chieftainship, the sakau, the feasts — isn't something you visit. 
It's something that's still happening.
```

**Why this is better:**
- Opens with the name meaning as a single strong line (not buried in a parenthetical)
- "Thirteen miles across, surrounded by reef, and almost always under rain" — gives you the physical picture in one sentence
- "the wettest place in the Pacific" — this is more memorable and more accurate than "350 inches of rainfall" (which most people can't visualize)
- "so thick you can hear it breathe" — warm, sensory, personal. This is the local-guide voice.
- "The culture isn't something you visit. It's something that's still happening." — this is the line that separates Pohnpei from every museum-culture tourism pitch.
- Drops "Enjoy the Purity of Pohnpei" — reads like a tagline committee output

**Stats row:** Keep the four stats (1,606 visitors, 50+ experiences, 25% land conserved, 30% reefs protected). They work. Consider adding context to the visitor number: **"1,606 visitors in 2025 — one of the least-visited islands in the Pacific."** That turns a small number into a feature.

## SECTION 4 — NAN MADOL FEATURE

### Problem
This section repeats almost everything from the Nan Madol chapter card above. Same dates, same "basalt logs and coral fill," same "Saudeleur Dynasty."

### Fix — make it experiential, not encyclopedic
```
Nan Madol

You get there by boat. The channel narrows, the mangroves close in, 
and then the walls appear — massive basalt columns stacked six 
centuries ago on artificial islands in the middle of a lagoon.

Nan Madol was the seat of the Saudeleur Dynasty, a political and 
ceremonial complex built across 92 islets connected by tidal canals. 
It's now a UNESCO World Heritage Site — and still one of the most 
difficult-to-reach major archaeological sites on Earth.

That's part of what makes it worth it.

[Plan a visit to Nan Madol →]
```

**Why this is better:**
- Starts with the visitor experience ("You get there by boat") instead of dates
- Adds a specific detail (92 islets) that the current copy doesn't have
- "still one of the most difficult-to-reach" — turns the access challenge into appeal
- "That's part of what makes it worth it" — warm, confident, local voice
- CTA points toward trip planning, not `#dispatch`

## OVERALL STRUCTURE (top to bottom)

1. **Nav** — The Island / Experiences / Plan / Dispatch
2. **Hero** — headline + subtext + two CTAs + two images
3. **About** — name meaning + geography + culture intro + stats row
4. **Four Ways In** — chapter cards (Nan Madol, Wild Interior, Outer Atolls, Living Culture)
5. **Nan Madol Feature** — experiential deep-dive (not repeated from above)
6. **Start Here** — practical trip-planning strip (NEW)
7. **The Dispatch** — newsletter signup
8. **Marquee** — place name ticker
9. **Footer** — your branding, links, attribution

This flow takes someone from wonder → understanding → "okay how do I actually go" → staying connected. That's the full funnel on one page.
